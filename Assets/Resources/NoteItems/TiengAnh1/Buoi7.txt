	Critical Thinking in the Modern World

1. Definition of Critical Thinking
- Critical thinking is the ability to analyze, evaluate, and make reasoned judgments about information or arguments.
- It is not about criticizing everything but about questioning assumptions, seeking evidence, and making logical conclusions.

2. Importance in daily life
- In the digital era, people are exposed to a flood of information every day.
- Without critical thinking, one can easily fall into misinformation, propaganda, or manipulation.
Critical thinking helps individuals make better choices in education, career, and personal life.

3. Key elements of critical thinking
- Questioning – always ask “Why?”, “How?”, “What if?”.
- Evidence-based reasoning – base decisions on data and facts, not emotions or rumors.
- Open-mindedness – willing to consider different viewpoints before making a conclusion.
- Reflection – evaluate your own thinking process, identify biases and errors.

4. Application in modern society
- In education: helps students learn deeply, not just memorize.
- In the workplace: supports problem-solving, creativity, and innovation.
- In citizenship: allows people to participate responsibly in social and political issues.

5. Conclusion
- Critical thinking is a lifelong skill, essential for personal growth and for building a democratic, innovative, and resilient society.
