	Ho Chi Minh’s Thought on the Communist Party of Vietnam

1. The Party as the decisive factor of victory
- Ho Chi Minh affirmed that the Communist Party of Vietnam was the leading force of the revolution.
- The Party was both the vanguard of the working class and the representative of the entire nation.
- Its ideological foundation must be Marxism–Leninism, which provided the scientific worldview and revolutionary methodology.

2. The Party’s relationship with the people
- The Party could only exist and develop thanks to the support of the people.
- Ho Chi Minh always reminded that the Party is “the servant of the people, not their master.”
- Every Party member had to keep close contact with the masses, listen to them, and put their interests above personal gains.

3. Party ethics and morality
- Revolutionary morality was considered the root of the Party.
- Ho Chi Minh required cadres and Party members to practice “diligence, thrift, integrity, uprightness, and absolute selflessness.”
- Only by being clean and honest could the Party truly lead the nation.

4. Principles of Party organization
- Democratic centralism: combining broad democracy with strict discipline.
- Self-criticism and criticism: a tool for correcting mistakes, strengthening unity, and keeping the Party healthy.
- Strict discipline and unity of will were essential for leadership.

5. Summary
- Ho Chi Minh’s thought on the Communist Party can be summed up in three key aspects:
- The Party as the leader of the revolution.
- The Party as a moral, clean, and ethical organization.
- The Party as deeply connected to the people it serves.
