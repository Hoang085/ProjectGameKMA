	Topic: Daily Routines & Habits

1. Vocabulary Expansion
- wake up – thức dậy
- get up – ra khỏi giường
- brush teeth – đánh răng
- take a shower – tắm
- have breakfast / lunch / dinner – ăn sáng / trưa / tối
- go to school / go to work – đi học / đi làm
- do homework – làm bài tập về nhà
- watch TV / listen to music / play games – xem TV / nghe nhạc / chơi game
- go shopping – đi mua sắm
- go to bed – đi ngủ

2. Grammar Focus
- We use the Present Simple tense to talk about routines and habits.
- Affirmative:
	I wake up at 6:30 every day.
	She goes to school by bus.
- Negative:
	I don’t get up late on weekdays.
	He doesn’t watch TV at night.
- Questions:
	Do you usually eat breakfast at home?
	What time does she go to bed?
	Use Adverbs of Frequency to describe how often something happens: always (100%), usually (80%), often (60%), sometimes (40%), rarely (20%), never (0%).
- Examples:
	I always brush my teeth before breakfast.
	They sometimes play football after school.
	She never drinks coffee at night.

3. Reading Practice
- This is Anna’s daily routine.
- Anna usually wakes up at 6:45 in the morning. She gets up and takes a shower. After that, she has breakfast with her family. At 7:30, she goes to school by bicycle. Anna usually has lunch at school with her friends. In the afternoon, she does her homework and sometimes plays badminton in the park. In the evening, Anna often watches TV or reads books. She goes to bed at 10:00.

4. Practice Sentences
- I usually wake up early, but on Sundays I get up late.
- My father always drinks tea in the morning.
- Do you often go shopping at the weekend?
- We never play football at night.

5. Mini Dialogue
	A: What time do you usually wake up?
	B: I wake up at 7 o’clock. How about you?
	A: I wake up earlier, at 6 o’clock, because I like to go jogging.
	B: That’s healthy! I usually go jogging in the evening instead.
